How BlueFoodOS Makes Money

1. Farmer Subscriptions Trial (₦0) → Active (₦5K/cycle) → Premium (₦25K/month/cluster)
2. Feed Procurement Margin 5-8% margin on bulk feed contracts (₦500K+/month)
3. Cold Chain as a Service ₦200/kg harvest storage + transport (₦100K+/month/site)
4. Cage Financing 2-3% financing fee on ₦2M-5M cage deals
5. Bank Commission 1-2% origination fee on ₦100K-500K farmer loans (₦50K+/month)
6. Data Analytics Premium data/API access for feed mills & exporters

Investor Questions

What's your go-to-market?

USSD-first + WhatsApp. Farmers buy directly. We pilot clusters with local associations and Lagos state partners.

What's the CAC & LTV?

CAC: ₦2K-5K (referral + SMS). LTV: ₦200K+ (3-year subscription + procurement). LTV:CAC = 40-50x.

What's your churn rate?

Pilot cohort: 4% monthly churn. Mostly due to relocations. NPS: +72. Low involuntary churn.

Who are your competitors?

No direct competitor in Nigeria. eFishery (Indonesia) is inspiration, not competition. We're earlier and more B2B-focused.

What's your burn rate?

Pilot phase: ₦5M/month. Runway: 18 months on current cap table. Breakeven at 10 clusters (₦4M revenue/month).

When is your next raise?

Series A: Q3 2026 ($2M target). We're currently in pre-seed conversations. Interested? Let's talk.

Where else can you scale?

Ghana, Côte d'Ivoire, Cameroon have similar gaps. Same product, local ops. But Nigeria first.

How does regulation affect you?

CBN supportive of agritech. FinTech framework applies. We're compliant. No major regulatory risk identified.
